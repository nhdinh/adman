<objective>
Close three related gaps in the gate's group-membership and refusal-surface paths, all touching `Invoke-AdmanMutation.ps1`:

**G-02-8 (major):** The D-04 remediation asymmetry is dead code. The gate runs `Test-AdmanTargetAllowed` on the member for ALL group verbs, and the member-side protected-membership check (step d) is not operation-aware. For `Remove-ADGroupMember` from a protected group, the protected-membership refusal should be skipped (the membership IS the state being remediated); other member-side checks (deny-RID, scope) should still apply. UAT Test 12 Leg 2 showed Remove-AdmanGroupMember of uat-protected1 from Domain Admins refused with 'recursive member of protected group' — remediation can never succeed.

**G-02-9 (minor):** The group-side Refused audit record for Add-ADGroupMember carries the group DN in both target and group fields; the member DN (who was being added) is absent — forensics cannot tell which member the add was attempted on. Root cause: gate writes the group-refusal audit with `-Target $groupObj` (lines 123-124) before per-member records exist.

**G-02-6 (minor):** Refusal reasons are not surfaced to the operator. The gate early-return path (lines 148-158) emits no Write-Warning/throw carrying the aggregated refusal reasons; the operator sees only `Denied: 1` on screen and cannot tell why.

Purpose: Restore the D-04 remediation asymmetry (a core Tier-0 cleanup workflow), make the audit record forensically complete, and surface refusal reasons to the operator.

Output: `Test-AdmanTargetAllowed` gains an optional `-Operation` parameter; `Invoke-AdmanMutation` passes it for group verbs, restructures the group-refusal audit to name the member DN, and emits Write-Warning on the early-return path. Two new Pester test files prove the three behaviors.
</objective>
